Research Vision & Trajectory

Enabling Intelligent Systems That
Operate Reliably Under Uncertainty

My research programme builds from foundational contributions in geospatial uncertainty modeling and knowledge discovery (Ph.D., Rennes 1; HDR, Manouba) through scalable deep learning for remote sensing and IoT security (distributed CNN architectures, privacy-preserving pipelines, federated learning) toward a current and forward-looking agenda in agentic AI, edge intelligence, and trustworthy autonomous systems.


The unifying thread is enabling intelligent systems to operate reliably under uncertainty, at scale, and with privacy guarantees — spanning environmental monitoring, precision agriculture, healthcare, and defense. As Director of the RIOTU Laboratory, I am building the research infrastructure and international collaborations needed to lead large-scale, multi-investigator programmes in decentralized agentic intelligence for IoT and unmanned systems.

Signature Research Contributions

Five Lines of Work That Changed
How We Approach These Problems

1

Uncertainty-Aware Geospatial Intelligence

Developed a principled framework for propagating and reducing epistemic/aleatory uncertainties in land cover change models. Published in IJAEOG (IF 7.5), Ecological Informatics, KAIS, J. Comp. Science.

 Spawned 3 doctoral theses extending this methodology
2

Privacy-Preserving Deep Learning for Satellite & Health Imagery

Pioneered hybrid differential-privacy + federated learning pipelines for VHR satellite classification and COVID-19 chest X-ray diagnosis — proving privacy guarantees need not degrade accuracy.

 Directly applicable to defense and health sectors
3

Distributed Deep Learning at Scale

Designed RS-DCNN, a distributed CNN overcoming single-machine memory limits (Comp. Elect. Agriculture, IF 8.6), complemented by CNN-LSTM urban expansion prediction (Ecol. Informatics, IF 5.9).

 Both among top-cited papers in their respective journals
4

AI-Driven Precision Agriculture: Red Palm Weevil Detection

Created the first deep-learning system for early RPW detection via acoustic signals (CEA, IF 8.6) and UAV-based palm detection. Saudi patent (SAIP SA 18938 B1, 2024).

 Under commercial field evaluation — addressing a pest costing hundreds of millions annually
5

Field-Shaping Surveys as Standard References

Highly cited surveys in Computer Science Review (IF 12.9) on IoT + Big Data for environment, DL for smart cities, graph DL for remote sensing, and LLMs + diffusion models (IF 12.7).

 Collectively among the most cited papers in their journals worldwide
